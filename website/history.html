Listening History

üíΩ 1/8/2025 - Mars Audiac Quintet by Stereolab Need to find more female vocalists who sound like this. I love the sing-songy but really chill vocals... It's a really simple sound that I prefer to alot of more... to put it poorly... soul/gospel influenced vocals that you tend to hear, especially these days. Lots of "ornamentation" I guess. It's either that or the cursive singing that I can't stand!! Anywho, I'm getting off track. I really dig how this band sounds. It sounds how those caricatures of hippies and their aesthetics looked in cartoons. But it's also still got that distinctive 90s warmth.... I love it!!!
üíΩ 11/3/2024 - Six Desperate Ballads by The Garden The only songs I know by The Garden are Call This # Now and Thy Mission. I think I listened to one of their albums before and was expecting an upbeat silly alt rock thing, so this sorta weird art-punk experimentation came as a bit of a surprise. I don't really mess with it though... Most of the songs have potential but are just too messy for me to enjoy. I also can't believe how much I disliked Hell Hole; it's not often I decide I never want to hear a song again?? That being said, I did like the last two tracks a fair amount. These guys certainly have range, I just think they might need to find their footing... but who knows, since everyone else seems to like the album??
üéµ 10/18/2024 - M3 N MIN3 by Femtanyl and Danny Brown Is 2024 just the year of great music or something?! 2024 and 2023! I was already loving life when I saw Danny Brown gassing up Femtanyl, and now the worlds collide where they made a collab?! I'm in love! I'm surprised how poorly this song was received, but maybe it's just from people who don't dig Femtanyl in the first place. This is one of the few times I wish the mixing made the vocals more coherent, because rap is kinda one of those things where you wanna understand what the person is saying... Still, could this signify an upcoming collab album....?!

üéµ 9/24/2024 - Enemy of My Enemy by All Them Witches This goes WAYYYY harder than I would have expected from All Them Witches??? üíΩ 8/15/2024 - I LAY DOWN MY LIFE FOR YOU by JPEGMAFIA You guys have no idea how badly I needed this. To me, Peggy was one of those artists that I always felt had crazy good potential, but I just had to wait it out for him to find his footing. I admired his experimental, chaotic sound but it didn't appeal to me too much outside of a few songs. I thought the Danny Brown collab really transformed his style, and we have the evolution I've been waiting for on this album, and it's harder than I ever could've imagined!! You're telling me this dude is taking some already off-kilter hip-hop beats, throwing some distorted guitar riffs over it, and then has the audacity to add in STRINGS for emotional weight?! This dude does practically everything I love in a song to such an insane extent that it feels like every song is secretly 3 different micro-songs. When people say hip-hop is the new punk, JPEGMAFIA is who they're talking about.
üíΩ 5/25/2024 - DARK TIMES by Vince Staples I'll admit I really like Vince Staples' earlier music the most out of his overall catalog. I tend to like that loud bombastic sound... Nothing wrong with depressing... it's just not my cup o' tea lately. Maybe I should go back and relisten to his last two records. Maybe I was being too harsh on them?
